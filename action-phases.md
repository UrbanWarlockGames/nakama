# Action Phases

The game is played through Action Phases. Each Scene occurs in one Action Phase. When the focus of play changes, the Scene ends and a new Scene begins in a different Action Phase.

Narrative Mode focuses on exploration, investigation, skill challenges, and social interactions. Abilities are abstract and Scenes are framed by the GM and resolved by the players.

Negotiation Mode focuses on resolving disputes through reasoning, empathising, and moralising. Abilities are not used and Scenes are framed by the GM and resolved by the players using what they know about the character they are negotiating with.

Tactical Mode focuses on precise positioning, . Abilities and Actions are precise and defined by their Tactical Actions. Scenes are framed by the GM and resolved as conflict between the GM and players.

Downtime Mode focuses on winding down, the aftermath of a mission, your changing Bonds with your Nakama, and dealing with the Pushes and Pulls of your Vices and Virtues.

## Narrative Mode
While Tactical Mode is defined by explicitly what an Ability or Action says it can do, Narrative Mode is defined by how your character interacts with the world, using their Drives, Vices, and Virtues to drive the Scene, and how your character feels about the world. In Narrative Mode, rolls are made only when the outcome would meaningfully change the direction of the Scene.

- Weak Hit: Move forward with a Complication or Cost, such as owing a favour or taking extra time or making noise -- anything that raises the stakes of the Scene. May also involve your Vices or Virtues getting in the way of the Scene.
- Hit: Move forward with your Drives (Motivations, Fears, Desires) guiding you.
- Strong Hit: Move forward with momentum, perhaps with the other party suffering from a Complication.

## Downtime (Slice of Life)
Downtime Mode is a distinct action phase taking place over the course of one or more days. The Players define a Scene and how they interact with and support each other after the action is resolved, such as between missions or while travelling together. Some example Scenes could be: walking home together, going fishing, shopping, watching fireworks, riding the train, cleaning weapons, stargazing, visiting a shrine, or playing board games.

Downtime starts with the **Relax Together** activity, then each Nakama has one Downtime action they can take. Downtime then ends with the **Rest** activity.

## Relax Together

Decide together what leisure activity the group is doing to let off steam, such as *visiting a hot spring*, *going to the beach*, *shopping for supplies*, *visiting a festival*, or *sharing a quiet evening at camp*. Each Player describes how they prepare for the Scene, what they are doing, and are free to interact with each other once everybody is finished. It is encouraged that everybody do one thing to contribute to the preparations, such as *paying the entrance fee*, *getting ingredients for dinner*, or *cleaning up after*.

The Players take turns asking each other one question:
- What is creating distance between us?
- What do you think I need to learn?
- What do you think will happen to the group?
- What do you admire about me?
- When did I disappoint you?
- What would make you leave?
- What are you running from?
- What do you wish someone understood about you?
- What do you think happens after this journey ends?
- Who here worries you most?

Or another personal question the asker would like to know, such as about their Nakama's life, goals, or dreams.

If you answer the question in an honest and meaningful way, you recover 1 Stress and the group's Fellowship pool increases by 1. If you do not, you may take the Ask For Help action after the Scene, or someone may Reach Out / Call you Out.

Each teammate should talk about a different Party member until no one is left out.

## Reach Out / Call Out

One Player becomes the Initiator.

Describe how your character notices something about another Nakama or decides to confront an issue between you.

Use Reach Out (Trust) when you offer comfort, patience, honesty, forgiveness, support, or protection.

Use Call Out (Antagonism) when you challenge, provoke, confront, compete with, or refuse to let another Nakama avoid something important.

State something specific your character wants from them, such as:
- understanding
- forgiveness
- respect
- trust
- honesty
- support
- encouragement
- an explanation
- a promise
- recognition

The Initiator must make themself vulnerable first.

This vulnerability may take many forms:
- Revealing a related Fear, Desire, or Need
- Admitting failure / they were wrong
- Admitting envy, fear, shame, or resentment
- Asking for help
- Revealing a Vice
- Revealing a Secret

Then, the target chooses their response.

### Let Them In

The target accepts the emotional connection.

To Let Them In, they must also make themselves vulnerable by explaining what Drive or Vice is causing their behaviour.

If both participants genuinely and meaningfully engage:

Choose two:
- Both recover 1 Stress
- Both recover all lost Belief
- Gain +1 Trust / +1 Antagonism
- Clear Courtesy Owed / Triumph between the participants

The scene should leave both characters changed.

### Rebuke

The target refuses the connection.

Examples:

- Deflect with humour
- Deny the problem / that they need help
- Change the subject
- Walk away
- Shut down emotionally
- Lie about their feelings
- Mock the Initiator for caring
- Insist they can handle it alone

The target retreats into their Vice and gains 1 Luck.

The Initiator gains 1 Influence on the target.

The relationship changes.

Then choose one consequence that fits the Scene:

- Antagonism between them increases by 1.
- Both suffer 1 Belief damage.
- The target gains 1 Stress.
- The target owes the Initiator a Courtesy.
- The Initiator gains 1 Stress.

The scene should reveal why the connection failed.

## Ask For Help

When Stress exceeds your Belief, you may Ask For Help. Ask For Help is a Downtime use of Ask a Favour between Nakama. Use it when you need emotional support, practical care, protection, honesty, forgiveness, intervention, reassurance, or help resisting a Vice, Fear, or Despair.

State what you need from your Nakama.

Examples:

- Stay with me.
- Stop me if I try to indulge in my Vice.
- Help me apologise.
- Tell me what I need to hear.
- Remind me why we are doing this.
- Do not let me run away.
- Help me face this Fear.
- Forgive me.
- Believe in me.
- Keep me from doing something I will regret.

The Nakama being asked decides the Favour Rank, based on what the request would cost them, risk for them, or require from them emotionally.

Normal Favour rules apply. If they accept, resolve the Favour normally, including Trust, Courtesy Owed, Debt, and any other consequences. If they refuse, the asker may request a Nakama Negotiation. If they refuse to Negotiate, the Favour is let go. 

Suggested Favour Ranks:

|   Rank | Ask For Help                                                                                                            |
| -----: | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
|      1 | Stay with me for a few minutes. Walk me home. Tell me I am not alone.                                                   |
| 2 to 3 | Spend the night making sure I do not act on my Vice. Help me apologise. Tell me the truth even if it hurts.             |
| 4 to 5 | Risk your reputation to protect me. Stand against someone you care about for me. Help me even if it betrays your Drive. |
|      6 | Give up something life-changing to save me from myself. Help me even though it may destroy what you believe in.         |

If the Favour is accepted and meaningfully helps the asker:
- The asker recovers 1 Stress.
- The asker restores Belief equal to the helper's Leadership.
- The helper clears Courtesy Owed if the accepted Favour is equal to or greater than the relevant Trust Rank.

This action should usually be accepted when the request is within the Bond and does not violate the helper's Drives. Refusal means the request exceeds the Bond, conflicts with a Drive, triggers a Vice, creates an unacceptable cost, or needs Negotiation. Refusal may Pull you to Overindulge in your Vice.

## Despair Scene

When your Stress exceeds your Belief, or when the table agrees your character is spiralling after severe Stress, failure, betrayal, or loss, you may frame a Despair Scene during Downtime.

Describe how your character withdraws, lashes out, shuts down, runs away, or turns to a Vice.

A Nakama may enter the Scene and attempt to Reach Out or Call you Out. If you Let Them In, resolve that action normally. If you Rebuke them and continue Overindulging, mark Luck from Overindulge and apply the normal Overindulge consequences.

## Overindulge in a Vice

You may Overindulge in a Vice as a Downtime Action or as part of a Despair Scene. If you do so, you cannot Uphold a Virtue this Downtime Episode.

When you Overindulge, explain what you do, what pushed you toward it, and why your Nakama cannot easily stop you. The Vice should meaningfully hinder you, hurt someone, create a complication, waste time, damage a Bond, or pull you away from what matters.

If another Nakama tries to stop you, they may Reach Out, Call Out, or Ask For Help if appropriate. If you reject them and continue Overindulging, resolve the rejection as Rebuke.

After the Scene:
- Mark Luck
- Gain 1 Stress
- Take a -1 penalty to your next roll using the Vice's paired Skill

You cannot have more than 1 Luck marked. If you already have Luck marked, you do not mark another. Luck disappears at the end of the next Episode.

## Uphold a Virtue

You may Uphold a Virtue as a Downtime Action or in Narrative Mode when your Virtue's Compulsion pushes you toward meaningful action. If you do so, you cannot Overindulge this Downtime Episode.

Explain what duty you are answering, who or what it is for, and what it costs you. The action must require effort, vulnerability, sacrifice, risk, or restraint. You cannot Uphold a Virtue by doing something easy, safe, or purely symbolic.

After the Scene, recover all lost Belief. If your Belief is full, restore 1 Fellowship. If one of your Nakama meaningfully assists you, they recover an amount of Belief equal to your Leadership score.

If Upholding your Virtue creates trouble, puts you at risk, or forces a hard choice, the GM may also allow the party to restore 1 Fellowship even if your Belief was not full.

## Rest

A Rest is an eight hour minimum period of leisure and minimal productivity. You may talk to your Nakama, building Trust or Antagonism with each other, Ask a Favour of a Nakama, or Rouse a Rival to action.

To receive healing benefits from a Rest, your Nakama must put together a meal of ingredients each of you procure. When adding an ingredient, each Nakama shares a new memory, dream, Secret, Drive, or wish connected to the ingredient. Doing so restores the group's Fellowship by 1 and restores Belief equal to the speaker's Leadership score.

The party member with the highest Cooking skill adds the spices and any finishing touches to put the meal together. If you are following a known recipe or all Nakama agree the food sounds like "Good Food", you may get a +1 bonus. This must be a new meal with new ingredients each Rest to gain the full benefits.

- Weak Hit: You create a filling meal. It does not taste the best, but it is filling. Each member who partakes in the meal restores an amount of Health equal to their level. Their Stamina is also fully restored.
- Hit: You create Good Food. In addition to the Weak Hit effects, each party member's Belief is fully restored.
- Strong Hit: You create Wonderful Food. In addition to the Hit effects, everyone's Health is fully restored. If the party's Health is full, each Nakama gains a +1 forward on one Skill of the chef's choice.
