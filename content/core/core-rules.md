Nakama: Battle Prowess is a tactical tabletop role-playing game with slice of life elements inspired by battle shounen anime.

## Checks

Roll a number of `d6` equal to your `Attribute + Skill/Strike/Power` when a character attempts something risky, or the outcome is uncertain.

:::archive title="Note: Rounding"
Remember: Always round down!
:::

## Hit Tiers

After rolling, count the number of 6s. If there are no 6s present, you rolled a Weak Hit. If there is exactly 1 6 present, you rolled a Hit. If there are 2+ 6s present, you rolled a Strong Hit. However, if exactly 3 6s are present, your Strong Hit becomes a Messy Critical.

- Weak Hit: You either succeed at a cost or fail at the task. You get a +1 forward on your next check with the associated Skill, if it's a Skill check, and the Tension increases by 1.
- Hit: You succeed at your task with.
- Strong Hit: You succeed at the task and create an advantage for yourself or an opening for your Nakama.
- Messy Critical: You get caught up in the moment and succeed a little *too well* at what you were trying to accomplish.

## Clocks

The GM may set a **Progress Clock** with 4-6 segments to track events that cannot be resolved in a single roll. Each time a Player rolls a Weak Hit, fill a segment of the Clock. The goal of the Players is to succeed at their task before all segments are filled.

## Maps

Nakama: Battle Prowess can be played either with a map using squares or gridless, though some modifications may be required to facilitate gridless play, such as using 1-2 enemies of equal or Level + 1 instead of 3-4 enemies and a group of Minions and de-emphasising Range and Distance. Otherwise, tracking may become difficult, especially if the map size is also not constrained.

In normal gridded play, Nakama assumes a 10x10 or smaller square grid. Hexes are usable with some changes to Areas and Size (count hexes adjacent to a point centred on a hex for odd numbers or centred on an intersection of hexes for even numbers).
