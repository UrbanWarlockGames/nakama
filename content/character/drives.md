Your Drives are what keep your character going on their path towards their Wish.

When you clear a Drive, mark 1 XP and replace it with a new Drive.

For example, you may get over your *Fear of making mistakes*, but following the death of losing one of your Nakama, you may develop a *Fear of watching someone die*.

## Drives

- Fear: Something you *fear* or *hate*.
- Desire: Something *selfish* you want or would rather be doing right now.
- Need: Something you *need* to survive, such as food, water, shelter, money.

## Debate

Each Negotiation trait has a Resistance Value: Vulnerable, Neutral, or Resist. The lowest Debate Rating is Vulnerable. The middle Debate Rating is Neutral. The highest Debate Rating is Resist. You choose the rating when there is a tie between one or more Debate Ratings. Debate Rating is used when another character targets one of your Negotiation traits. See Negotiation Mode.

- Ethics: Ethos = Authority; Law, Duty; what I have to do, what I ought to do (otherwise...)
- Empathy: Pathos = Empathy; Morality; what I feel is right
- Logic: Logos = Deduction; Evidence-based Reasoning; what I think should be done, what the clues point to

## Motivations

Make a Statement for your Wish, Long-term Goal, and at least two Short-term Goals.

Your Motivations must be achievable, specific, quantified, personal, and require effort to attain.

You accomplish a Long-term Goal roughly once per Arc (4-6 Episodes) and each Short-term Goal by the end of 1-2 Episodes (2-3 or 4-6 Sessions).

When you accomplish a Long-term Goal, mark 2 XP or PP progress. When you accomplish a Short-term Goal, mark 1 XP or PP progress.

### Wish

Your Wish is where you want to be by the end of the game. It's your primary goal for setting out across the land and is deeply personal to you. It is also extremely difficult to achieve or may take years of effort, or even be outright impossible without magic.

Once you accomplish your Wish, your time travelling with your Nakama is likely over, but you may still be compelled by the power of your Bonds for a short while.

## Vices and Virtues

A Vice represents a broad description of one of your fatal flaws.

A Virtue represents a broad description of one of thing you believe is the right thing to do.

Choose or create three Vices and Virtues. Pair each to a Skill, and then choose a Compulsion Trigger for that Skill. This is your **Compulsion**, an otherworldly Pull towards your Vice. How you interpret your Vice, and whether you choose to act on it is up to you. If you or the GM decide resisting temptation would require a roll, it is called a Push instead. For a Push, roll [Willpower] + [paired Skill].

For example, you may make your Vice: **Drunkard (Composure)** with the Compulsion Trigger **Visit a bar** or **See an unattended alcoholic drink**. When the opportunity to indulge arises, you may declare your intended action. If your GM agrees it would hinder your party or hurt you to act on your Compulsion, you mark Ego upon completing the action. Ego acts like Fellowship, but can only be used by you. You can only have one point of Ego marked, and it goes away at the end of the next Episode.

You may also Overindulge as a Downtime Action. If you choose to Overindulge in your Vice, explain what you do and what pushes your character to do it. After the Scene, you gain a -1 to your next roll using the paired Skill and gain one Stress. Regardless of whether you Request/Accept a Compulsion from the GM or Overindulge, you mark Ego.

Virtues are a *moral duty* you feel towards a *person, place, thing, or cause*. The Compulsion Trigger can be as general or specific as you like, such as: **Never let a friend down** or **Liberate the oppressed from tyranny**. You may Uphold your Virtue in Narrative Mode or as a Downtime Action. See Uphold a Virtue.
