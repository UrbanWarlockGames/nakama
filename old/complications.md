## Complications

After the Complication is chosen or rolled, the GM reads the Complication out-loud and asks, "Does your character know what that means?" This gives the Player the freedom to interpret their Complication in an interesting or character-focused manner if they desire. The GM is encouraged to "yes, and" the response and build on top of it, if it is not quite meaningful enough of a Complication, rather than outright denying it. If the Player declines, the GM is free to interpret the Complication however they wish. Other Players are also encouraged to build on the Complication if they have a good idea, but it is not required. If a Complication is ever not fitting, the GM can re-roll it any time.

### A Deal with the Devil

Upon making any roll, a Player may request A Deal with the Devil. They may take one of the following benefits:
- Increase the Hit Tier of the roll
- Recover an amount of Stamina equal to the highest of their Level or half their maximum Stamina
- A special offer or request from the GM

During a Deal with the Devil, the GM gives the Player two interpretations of a Complication to choose from.

### d20 Complications
This chart may be rolled if the player is asked to roll a Complication or if the GM is unable to improv a fitting Complication.
- 1: A powerful creature takes notice.
- 2: An authority figure steps in.
- 3: Something important is broken.
- 4: An ally makes things worse.
- 5: A bystander gets involved.
- 6: A rival enters the Scene.
- 7: An enemy gains an advantage.
- 8: A hidden threat is revealed.
- 9: A misunderstanding occurs.
- 10: The environment turns hostile.
- 11: The situation becomes public, personal, or urgent.
- 12: A resource is lost, spent, damaged, or stolen.
- 13: A tool, weapon, ability, vehicle, or contact stops acting as expected.
- 14: A secret is threatened or revealed.
- 15: A party member's Vice is Invoked.
- 16: An existing threat advances.
- 17: Time or a clock advances.
- 18: A path becomes blocked.
- 19: A foe gathers strength.
- 20: ...
